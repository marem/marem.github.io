Academic Research Outputs

Published

Mar 2024

Doctoral Thesis

Maré, M. (2023). Influence of Behaviour Centred Design approaches on the diffusion of flame-based cookstoves among indigent South African households [1] Doctor of Technology: Design, Cape Peninsula University of Technology.
This research addresses the evidence gap in shifting inefficient culinary practices by utilizing a customized multi-phase research design framework to implement and evaluate behavioral interventions in low-income settings.

Academic Papers & Publications

2023

Influencing motivations linked to the adoption of improved flame-based cookstoves among indigent South African households: a behaviour-centred design approach [2]
Sustainability: Science Practice and Policy
This study validated a phase-based behavioral design framework (BCD) using emotional demonstrations and co-creative workshops to trigger a revaluation of cookstove-linked motivations.

2021

The influence of behaviour centred design approaches on the flame-based cookstove-related health and wellbeing of indigent South African households [3]
Proceedings of the 3rd Annual International Conference, Machakos University
This paper explores the role of motivational touchpoints in facilitating a shift in cooking behavior to improve health and quality of life in sub-Saharan households.

2018

Advancing a clean cookstove culture in sub-Saharan Africa: The transformative power of Afrikan innovation [4]
Proceedings of the 1st Annual International Conference, Machakos University
This research investigates the integration of culturally informed, participatory design methods rooted in African worldviews to shift energy-poor households toward safer technology.

2017

The use of Willingness to Pay in determining customer preferences for improved flame-based cookstove features in two South African study areas [5]
Social Marketing Quarterly
Using Discrete Choice Experiments (DCE), this study found that safety awareness campaigns significantly shift consumer demand from kerosene to efficient LPG stoves.

2016

User experience observations with improved flame-based cookstoves among a sample of South African households [6]
Proceedings of the LeNSes Conference
This paper utilizes qualitative, ethnographic field research and structured observations to identify specific service design goals for the uptake of energy technologies.

2014

Customer preferences for improved flame-based cookstove features in two South African study areas [7]
Proceedings of the 22nd Domestic Use of Energy (DUE) Conference
This study quantitatively maps specific attributes like fuel types, durability, and payment options against consumer purchase intent in urban and rural settings.

2013

The applicability of “willingness to pay” for the diffusion of energy-efficient domestic stove technologies in the Southern African market [8]
Proceedings of the 21st DUE Conference
This paper demonstrates that WTP surveys are a fundamental diagnostic tool for understanding market demand and barriers at the “base of the pyramid”.

2010

The applicability of social marketing in the uptake of bio-energy based technologies [9]
Proceedings of the People Energy Network (PEN) Conference
An early review evaluating the success of social marketing strategies, as opposed to commercial profit-driven approaches, in the dissemination of bio-energy technologies.
