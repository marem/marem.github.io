\subsubsection{Professional Summary}\label{professional-summary}

Marcel Maré researches, lectures, and consults at the intersection of
strategic design, energy and the behavioural sciences. He is an
established educator with over 15 years of experience in interaction
design and an extensive background in multimedia, energy studies, and
design education. With a robust trajectory in academia and the creative
industries, his diverse experience spans entrepreneurial endeavours in
the craft industry to collaborative projects with corporate clients and
local communities (\textbf{?@fig-vision}).

Holding a doctoral degree in Design, his work spans sustainable energy,
social marketing, and behaviour change. He has contributed extensively
to research in clean energy transition, strategic design, and community
engagement. Marcel's interdisciplinary approach integrates design
thinking with behavioural sciences to tackle large-scale societal
challenges (\textbf{?@fig-bakery}). As a lecturer, Marcel has developed
structured curricula, mentored research projects, and integrated
real-world business challenges into the learning process, equipping
students with both theoretical and professional skills
(\textbf{?@fig-TUT}).

Marcel's scholarly work centres on advancing sustainable energy
solutions in sub-Saharan Africa, with a specific focus on the strategic
design and widespread dissemination of improved cookstoves. His
interdisciplinary research approach integrates design thinking with
social marketing principles {[}1{]}.

\paragraph{Social Marketing and Consumer
Preferences}\label{social-marketing-and-consumer-preferences}

His work has contributed to understanding the role of social marketing
in the adoption of improved bio-energy technologies {[}1{]}. He has
further investigated consumer preferences for cookstove features in
South African regions, employing willingness-to-pay surveys to refine
marketing strategies for stove dissemination {[}2{]}, {[}3{]}, as
illustrated in Figure~\ref{fig-mamelodi}.

\paragraph{Service Design and Behavioural
Goals}\label{service-design-and-behavioural-goals}

He has explored the application of service design principles to identify
and implement behavioural goals that could boost the uptake of improved
cookstoves {[}4{]}, {[}5{]}. This approach signifies a shift from
product-focused strategies to comprehensive service design strategies,
emphasising human-centred design and observational field research.

\paragraph{Behavioural Change through Design
Approaches}\label{behavioural-change-through-design-approaches}

Since 2021 his research delves into the influence of Behaviour Centred
Design (BCD) on the health and wellbeing of indigent households in South
Africa {[}6{]}, {[}7{]}, {[}8{]}. As illustrated in
Figure~\ref{fig-bcd}, he examined how BCD approaches can modify
cookstove-related behaviours and address broader social, economic, and
environmental challenges.
